Chilli Con Carne

Chilli con carne combines mince, tomatoes and kidney beans into a spicy yet hearty stew, perfect for a cold winter day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large onion
  • 1 red pepper
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 500g lean minced beef
  • 1 beef stock cube
  • 400g canned chopped tomatoes
  • ½ tsp dried marjoram
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp tomato pureé
  • 410g canned red kidney beans

Method:

  1. Chop 1 large onion into small dice, about 5mm square.
  2. Cut 1 red pepper in half lengthways, remove stalk and wash the seeds away, then chop.
  3. Peel and finely chop 2 garlic cloves.
  4. Put your pan on the hob over a medium heat. Add 1 tbsp oil and leave it for 1-2 minutes until hot.
  5. Add the onion and cook, stirring fairly frequently, for about 5 minutes.
  6. Stir in the garlic, red pepper, 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp paprika and 1 tsp ground cumin.
  7. Leave it to cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Turn the heat up a bit, add the lean minced beef to the pan and break it up with a spoon.
  9. Keep stirring for at least 5 minutes, until all the mince is in uniform, mince-sized lumps and there are no more pink bits.
  10. Crumble 1 beef stock cube into 300ml hot water. Pour this into the pan with the mince mixture.
  11. Add in a 400g can of chopped tomatoes, ½ tsp dried marjoram, 1 tsp sugar and squirt in about 2 tbsp tomato pureé. Stir well.
  12. Bring the contents of the pan to a boil, give it a good stir and put a lid on the pan. Turn down the heat until it is gently bubbling and leave it for 20 minutes.
  13. Check on the pan occasionally to stir it and make sure the sauce is not drying out. If it is, add a couple of tablespoons of water and make sure that the heat is low enough.
  14. Drain and rinse a 410g can of red kidney beans in a sieve and stir them into the chilli pot. Bring to the boil again, and gently bubble without the lid for another 10 minutes, adding a little more water if it looks too dry.
  15. Season the pot with salt and pepper.
  16. Replace the lid, turn off the heat and leave the chilli to stand for 10 minutes before serving.
  17. Serve with soured cream and plain boiled long grain rice.
