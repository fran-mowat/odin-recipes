Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti carbonara is an Italian pasta dish which combines spaghetti, eggs, gammon and cheese into an simple, creamy dish.

Ingredients:

  • 120g dried spaghetti
  • 200g gammon
  • 30g Pecorino cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pack button mushrooms
  • ½ brown onion
  • 1 vegetable stock cube
  • ¼ tsp mustard powder
  • Cooking spray

Method:

  1. Chop the onion, mushrooms and gammon into small pieces.
  2. Place the spaghetti in a pan of boiling water to cook the pasta.
  3. Heat a large frying pan with a few sprays of cooking spray. Fry the onions until softened, add the mushrooms & gammon and cook till browned slightly.
  4. Add 300ml vegetable stock to the frying pan, and cook on high stirring frequently until it reduces by half.
  5. Whisk the eggs, black pepper and mustard in a separate bowl.
  6. Drain the spaghetti once it's cooked and tip into a big bowl and add the cooked onions, mushrooms, remaining stock and gammon, stirring well.
  7. Slowly add the whisked eggs and Pecorino cheese making sure to stir constantly.
  8. Serve with sprinkled parsley and more Pecorino cheese.
