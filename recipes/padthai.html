Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a dish of rice noodles with eggs, vegetables and prawns sautéed in a wok to form a delicious meal.

Ingredients:

  • 175g flat rice noodles
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 50g potted brown shrimp
  • 1 ½ tbsp vegetable oil
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • pinch of garlic salt
  • 20 large raw king prawns
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • ½ tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp sweet chilli sauce
  • 1 tbsp soft brown sugar
  • 2 tsp shrimp paste
  • 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 40g roasted peanuts
  • 4 spring onions
  • 100g fresh bean sprouts
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander

Method:

  1. Boil a large pan of water and add the noodles. Turn off the heat and leave to stand for 3-4 minutes before draining, and rinsing with cold water. Drizzle on the sesame oil and toss together.
  2. Heat a large wok over a high heat. Add the potted shrimp in butter, vegetable oil, salt and garlic salt. Fry for 2-3 minutes until lightly golden.
  3. Remove the shrimp with a spoon and place in a bowl - leaving the oil in the wok.
  4. Add the king prawns, garlic and dried chilli flakes to the oil in the wok. Fry for 2-3 minutes until the king prawns just start to turn pink.
  5. Whisk the eggs together and add to the prawns.
  6. Fry, stirring often with a spatula until the eggs are just scrambled.
  7. Turn down the heat to medium and add the noodles, potted shrimp, fish sauce, tamarind paste, sweet chilli sauce, sugar, shrimp paste and soy sauce.
  8. Toss everything together, heating for about 5 minutes, until the noodles are hot. Keep the noodles moving around the wok to stop them sticking.
  9. Add the peanuts, spring onions, beansprouts and coriander.
  10. Toss together and cook for a further 1-2 minutes, until the beansprouts are just heated through.
  11. Serve with lime wedges and top with fresh red chillies and chopped peanuts.
